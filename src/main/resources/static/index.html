Eat well to live better

Our eating habits largely determine the functioning of our body. A good diet adapted to our nutritional needs promotes health and prevents diseases. Healthy eating is about eating a variety of foods that give you the nutrients you need to stay healthy, feel good, and have energy. These nutrients include proteins, carbohydrates, fats, water, vitamins and minerals. A healthy diet must be varied, balanced, safe, adequate, complete and sufficient.

  • Complete: containing all the nutrients, include at least one food from each of the three groups in each meal.
  • Balanced: that the nutrients are in appropriate proportions to each other.
  • Sufficient: that covers the needs of all nutrients, so that there is good nutrition and a healthy weight.
  • Varied: include different foods from each group in meals.
  • Safe: its regular consumption does not imply health risks and is consumed in moderation.
  • Adequate: that is in accordance with the tastes and culture of each person, adjusted to their economic resources without sacrificing other characteristics.
